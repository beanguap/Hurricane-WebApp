Wind Speed

Category 1

Category 1 hurricanes are characterized by sustained winds of 74-95 mph. They can result in minimal damage, such as downed trees and power outages, and may cause some roof and siding damage to well-built homes. Coastal flooding and storm surges are possible but generally not severe.

Category 2

Category 2 hurricanes have winds of 96-110 mph. They can cause moderate damage, including roof and siding damage to homes, significant tree damage, and power outages. Coastal flooding and storm surges are more pronounced than in Category 1 storms.

Category 3

Category 3 hurricanes have winds of 111-129 mph. They can cause extensive damage, including severe damage to homes, uprooted trees, and extended power outages. Coastal flooding and storm surges are significant and can penetrate inland.

Category 4

Category 4 hurricanes have winds of 130-156 mph. They can cause catastrophic damage, including severe damage to well-built homes, uprooted trees, and long-lasting power outages. Coastal areas can experience extreme flooding and destructive storm surges.

Category 5

Category 5 hurricanes have winds exceeding 157 mph. They are extremely destructive, causing catastrophic damage to everything in their path, including well-built homes, uprooted trees, and prolonged power outages. Coastal areas suffer severe flooding and devastating storm surges.
